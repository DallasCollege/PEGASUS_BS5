Lucida Bright (.serif) should be limited to headers or content areas that need special attention.

For serif, we use Lucida Bright which should already be loaded on 76% of Windows based machines and 99% of Mac based machines.

font-family:'Lucida Bright', Georgia, serif;
Fallbacks

The fallback typefaces used in instances where Lucida Bright is unavailable are:

  • Georgia
Helper Class!

You can apply the .serif class to any HTML element (including heading elements) to transform the font to Lucida Bright.

Proper Use: Sans v. Serif

  • Open Sans should always be used for body copy
  • Lucida Bright should be limited to headers or content areas that need additional emphasis and not for body copy
  • When designing a page with lots of headers and header levels, you will find a need to have additional styles to help differentiate between <h2> and <h3>. Applying .serif to these headers is appropriate
